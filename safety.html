Safety & Ethics

Safety, ethics,
and responsible AI.

AI HealthGuide is a support tool — not a doctor, not a pharmacist, and never a replacement for professional care. Here is exactly how we approach safety, privacy, and the ethical use of AI in healthcare.

🚫

Not a medical professional — ever.

AI HealthGuide does not diagnose conditions, prescribe medication, or provide clinical advice. It simplifies and explains existing medical information — nothing more. Every response is AI-generated and may be incomplete, incorrect, or not applicable to your specific situation.

Always consult your GP, pharmacist, specialist, or other qualified healthcare professional before making any medical decision. If you are in a medical emergency, do not use this app — call 000 immediately.

Our principles

Six commitments to
responsible design

These principles guided every decision in building AI HealthGuide — from what it says, to what it refuses to do.

🚫

No Diagnosis

HealthGuide never attempts to diagnose a medical condition. It explains what terms and conditions mean — it does not determine whether a user has one. Clinical diagnosis requires a qualified professional.

🔒

Privacy Protection

A real deployment would use data minimisation, explicit user consent, encrypted storage, and strict access controls. In this prototype, no user data is stored, logged, or transmitted anywhere.

⚖️

Bias Mitigation

Healthcare AI must work equitably for everyone. A production system would require testing across age groups, cultural backgrounds, language levels, and medical literacy to identify and address bias.

👩‍⚕️

Human Oversight

AI should not operate unsupervised in healthcare. Critical information should be reviewed by healthcare professionals, and users should always have access to escalate to a human when needed.

🚨

Emergency Redirection

Detecting potential emergencies and directing users to call 000 is the single most important safety feature of this system. When in doubt, the response is always: call emergency services first.

💬

Transparency

Users are always clearly informed they are speaking with an AI, not a healthcare professional. Responses are honest about uncertainty, and limitations are communicated openly — never hidden.

Emergency response

How we handle emergencies

HealthGuide continuously scans every user message for keywords and phrases that may indicate a medical emergency. When detected, the chatbot immediately stops all other responses and directs the user to call 000.

There is no attempt to triage or advise — only a clear, urgent instruction to contact emergency services. No chatbot response is worth a delayed emergency call.

Detected keywords include (but are not limited to):

⚡ Chest pain
⚡ Can't breathe
⚡ Heart attack
⚡ Stroke
⚡ Unconscious

Privacy & data

What we do — and don't —
do with your data

Healthcare information is among the most sensitive data that exists. We take that seriously.

🧹

No data stored

This prototype stores nothing. Conversations exist only in your browser session and disappear when you close the tab. No messages are logged, saved, or transmitted to any server.

Prototype behaviour
🚫

No personal data collected

We do not collect names, email addresses, health identifiers, or any personally identifiable information. There are no accounts, no profiles, and no tracking cookies.

Current prototype
🔐

Real system requirements

A production deployment would require explicit informed consent, a published privacy policy, encrypted data at rest and in transit, data minimisation practices, and compliance with the Australian Privacy Act 1988.

For production use
📋

Sensitive health data

Under Australian law, health information is classified as sensitive data with additional protections. Any real healthcare AI system must comply with the My Health Records Act 2012 and relevant clinical data regulations.

Australian AI Ethics Framework

Grounded in responsible AI principles

HealthGuide's design aligns with Australia's AI Ethics Framework, developed by the Department of Industry, Science, Energy and Resources. These eight principles guide responsible AI development in Australia.

✓

Human, social & environmental wellbeing

AI should benefit individuals and society — HealthGuide's only purpose is patient wellbeing.

✓

Human-centred values

Respect for human dignity and autonomy — users are empowered with information, not dependent on AI decisions.

✓

Fairness

AI must not discriminate. HealthGuide aims to serve patients equally regardless of background or literacy.

✓

Privacy protection & security

Healthcare data is sensitive. A real system would apply strict data governance and security controls.

✓

Reliability & safety

Emergency detection and consistent disclaimers ensure that safety is never sacrificed for convenience.

✓

Transparency & explainability

Users always know they are talking to AI. Limitations are stated clearly, not buried in fine print.

✓

Contestability

Users should be able to challenge AI responses. HealthGuide consistently directs users back to professionals.

✓

Accountability

Designers and deployers of AI are responsible for its impacts. This project takes that obligation seriously.

🇦🇺

This project references the Australia's AI Ethics Framework (2019) published by the Australian Government Department of Industry, Science, Energy and Resources. The eight principles above are drawn directly from that framework as a guide for responsible AI development in the Australian context.

Honest limitations

What HealthGuide
cannot do

Being clear about limitations is as important as celebrating capabilities. Here is what this prototype does not — and should not — do.

✗

Not real AI

This prototype uses a rule-based pattern matching system, not a trained machine learning model. Responses are pre-written and triggered by keywords — it is not truly understanding your question.

✗

Not clinically validated

No healthcare professional has reviewed, validated, or endorsed the content of this chatbot. Explanations are simplified for general understanding and may not reflect the most current clinical guidance.

✗

Not for real medical use

This is a university assignment prototype built to demonstrate concepts. It is not a regulated medical device, not approved by the TGA, and must not be used to make real healthcare decisions.

✗

Limited knowledge

HealthGuide only responds to a small set of medications, conditions, and topics it was explicitly programmed with. It will not have useful answers for the vast majority of real medical questions.

✗

Cannot interpret context

The chatbot cannot consider your full medical history, current medications, allergies, or personal circumstances. Generic explanations may be misleading when applied to a specific situation.

✗

Not a substitute for human care

Empathy, clinical judgement, physical examination, and personal knowledge of a patient are things no current AI can replicate. HealthGuide is designed to complement care — never to replace it.
